About Me

I am an undergraduate student of Geography and History at Maynooth University, Ireland. My academic interests focus on Eastern Europe, with a growing emphasis on Russia and the wider Russian-speaking world. I am particularly interested in how history, geography, and language interact to shape identity and politics in this region.

Alongside my studies, I am actively involved in student leadership and societies, including the Maynooth Historical Society and the Students' Union. My long-term goal is to work as a Polish–English–Russian translator and interpreter, bridging communication across cultures.

Research & Academic Interests

My work explores the intersection of history, geography, and language in Eastern Europe, especially in relation to Russia. Key themes I am interested in include:

  • Historical geography of the former Soviet Union
  • Memory, identity, and historical narratives in Poland and Russia
  • Geopolitics and regional development in the Russian Federation
  • Comparative perspectives on Slavic cultures and languages

As my Russian improves, I aim to work directly with Russian-language primary sources and scholarship to deepen this research.

My Russian Language Journey

I began learning Russian at A1 level with the goal of integrating it into both my academic work and my future career in translation and interpreting. As a native Polish speaker and fluent English speaker, I am particularly interested in the connections and contrasts between Slavic and non-Slavic languages.

Current Focus:

  • Building a solid A2 foundation in grammar and core vocabulary
  • Daily self-study combining textbooks, online courses, and listening practice
  • Language exchange with native Russian speakers
  • Preparing for TORFL A2/B1 certification

In the future, I hope to study Russian language and culture at Moscow State University and reach B2 level, enabling me to use Russian confidently in academic and professional contexts.
